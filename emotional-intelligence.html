Emotional Intelligence

Emotional intelligence is one of the most important skills a person can develop.

Sometimes also known as Emotional Quotient (EQ), emotional intelligence is the ability to perceive, use, understand, manage, and handle emotions. This is different to Intelligence Quotient (IQ), which measures cognitive abilities like logic and problem-solving. In this post, I'll take a look at the differences between EQ and IQ, and explain why EQ is so important.

IQ vs EQ

Let's initially explore the difference between EQ and IQ. Both are important but, as I mentioned, they measure different things. IQ is a score derived from standardised tests that are designed to measure human intelligence. There are different proposed models to measure EQ - some require specific standardised testing, while others use self-report or 360-degree assessment. Because each model for measuring EQ is slightly different, they define the attributes of EQ differently. HelpGuide.org notes that EQ is commonly defined by four attributes:

  • Self-management – You're able to control impulsive feelings and behaviors, manage your emotions in healthy ways, take initiative, follow through on commitments, and adapt to changing circumstances.

  • Self-awareness – You recognize your own emotions and how they affect your thoughts and behavior. You know your strengths and weaknesses, and have self-confidence.

  • Social awareness – You have empathy. You can understand the emotions, needs, and concerns of other people, pick up on emotional cues, feel comfortable socially, and recognize the power dynamics in a group or organization.

  • Relationship management – You know how to develop and maintain good relationships, communicate clearly, inspire and influence others, work well in a team, and manage conflict.

So, why is EQ so important?

Generally, people with high EQ are better at managing their own emotions, which makes them less likely to become overwhelmed or stressed by challenging situations. They also tend to be better at communicating their feelings with other - which helps them develop stronger personal and professional relationships.

People with high EQ are better at empathizing with others. People with high levels of empathy can see things from another person's perspective, which makes them more able to understand their needs and motivations. This helps with relationship building and can lead to better interactions between people.

Finally, EQ is important because it can be developed and improved over time. IQ is generally considered to be determined by genetics and early childhood experiences, but EQ is a skill - able to be improved through practice and experience. Mindful development of EQ can make us more effective communicators, better leaders, and more fulfiled in our personal and professional lives.
